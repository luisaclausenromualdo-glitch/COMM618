**The Scale of the War in the Middle East in Five Maps**

At this point, I believe we can notice a trend in my interest for maps and data. In this case, Martin Gonzalez Gomez helps us understand the geographic scale of the war that the U.S. and Israel started with Iran. The article presents maps overlaying that region onto different parts of the world.
The article metnions the strikes carried out by both sides int he war have "stretched across a vast area of more than four million square miles." It then showed how that would look like by overlaying the map onto Europe. 
It also compared it with Ukraine, showing how Iran is almost three times the size of Ukraine and has more than double its population.
Although the article is "simple" and does disclose major revelations, I believe this to be the type of reporting that helps readers connect a major event in a foreign place where they don't know much about to something more tangible. 
International reporting can sometimes be complicated because people have a hard time caring about things that do not directly affect them. In this case, not only it shows how connected certain things are, but it also offers visuals for us to understand "What if it was with us? How much of our territory would it take up?"
