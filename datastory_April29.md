**Weapon offenses rise on Virginia university campuses**
https://vcij.org/stories/weapons-offenses-rise-on-virginia-university-campuses
This article by the Virginia Center of Investigative Journalism reports on the rise in weapons violations at Virginia college campuses. The story was written
in the wake of a deadly shooting in early March at Old Dominion University, where Lt. Col. Brandon Shah, head of ODU's ROTC program, was killed by a gun,an who had illegally purchased
a handgun shortly before the attack. The article looked into Virginia State Police data and found weapons law violations at these universities have climbed since 2020, reaching 55
reported violations by the end of 2025, nearly a 40% increase in five years. 
The article relies on a single source which covers 26 schools, with 15 reporting last year. That's a pretty small sample for statwide conclusions. 
The 40% figure needs some scrutiny because it went from 39 to 55 violations, which sounds a lot and dramatic as a percentahe, but the raw numbers are small enough that a few 
incidents can swing the rate wildly. The story does not help the reader evaluate the trend. The article also notes ODU accounts for roughly half of all aggravated assault cases, but does not dig into why.
There are also a lot of voices missing from the story, for example, not one student was quoted even though the story covers campus violence. There are many other things that 
have bothered me in this story, but the last thing I will say is how the title says "Weapon offenses rise on Virginia university campuses," and then we see on the second 
graph a decrease in weapon law violations. Overall, this story could have used more work and better structuring.
