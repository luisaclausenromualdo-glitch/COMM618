**Corporate America Is Minting Money—and Not Just in Tech and Finance**
The article covers how large U.S. corporations are posting strong profits despite a grim economic backdrop. Data shows the earnings per share growth is expected to exceed 13% for the sixth consecutive quarter, led heavily by tech, financials and materials. 
I like how this story flows well and builds credibility. Rather than vague claims about profits being "up," the piece anchors every assertion in numbers, for example highlighting the 13% EPS growth, United Airlines' profit rising 80%, American Express cardholder spending up 9% to a three-year high. These figures make the trend feel verifiable instead of just asserted.
The chart is clean and straightfoward. The S&P 500 earnings/revenue chart shows year-over-year trends across multiple quarters, letting readers see the trajectory rather than just a snapshot. I liked how it marked the "Liberation Day" tariffs and the Iran war as context, which helped connect the macro events to the data. 
I think one of the ways to improve it would be to have a consumer share their thoughts because the piece highlights how the consumer sentiment is darker than any previous crisis in 50 years. I understand that is not the main focus of the article but it could be a quick ad or a follow up.
