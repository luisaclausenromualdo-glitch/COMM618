
Washington, D.C. is on track for one of its safest years in recent memory, with one exception standing in the way.

Overall crime in the District is down 25% compared to the same period last year, according to data from the Metropolitan Police Department going through April 29. 
Homicides have dropped nearly in half, robberies are down 22% and motor vehicle theft, once among the city's most persistent problems, has plummeted 58%. If these trends hold, 2026 could mark the city's lowest crime totals in at least a decade.

But assault with a dangerous weapon is headed in the opposite direction. So far this year, assaults with dangerous weapons have surged 35%, rising from 266 in 2025 to 360 in the first four months of this year.
