**ICE Has Detained 6,200+ Kids in Trump’s Second Term, Up 10x Since Biden Left Office**

The Marshall Project's article is a strong example of data journalism that combines quantitative evidence with human testimony to build a layered, credible account of a concerning policy.
The article credits Deportation Data Project, a group of academics and lawyers who collect federal immigration data through public records requests and share it with the public. They found ICE has detained over 6,200 children during President Donald Trump's second term and compare with the Biden era when the daily average of detained children was 24 to the Trump era average of 226.
The comparison offered an effective way to use the data. The article also displays two line charts, one tracking children under 18 and another tracking the broader detained population. The visuals offered an effective way to show both the dramatic spike in January 2026 to over 550 and the subsequent sharp decline, which the article is addresses by noting the data after the low point "has not yet been made available."
Some could see the fact that the data ends on March 11 as a limitation. However, the fact that the article addresses it, encourages the reader to continue to stay up to date with future stories from the Marshall Project. 
The article notes over 1,600 children were held beyond the court-mandated 20-day limit. It could be interesting to learn how much longer were some of them held. Were most detained for 21-25 days or held there for months? That could be an interesting follow up or something to add because it adds more weight to the legal accountability angle the piece develops.
